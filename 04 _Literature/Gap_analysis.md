# Summary Gap Analysis: Today's SOC vs. My PhD Framework

This table explains the four academic gaps my project helps to fix, using clear and simple language.

| Gap Type | Gap Identified | Evidence (Citation) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Knowledge Gap** | SOC systems today look at alerts completely alone (like just checking one IP or log). They don't have real context, so they miss hidden connections and advanced attacks. | Chuvakin et al. (2024); Almgren & Jonsson (2021) |
| **Theoretical Gap** | Current security math and rules use simple "yes or no" logic (`IF score > 0.8 THEN block`). There are no complex formulas to understand normal server behavior and lag, which causes too many false alarms. | Shackleford (2024); NIST SP 800-61 Rev. 3 |
| **Methodological Gap** | Most university papers only test their AI code offline with old, static files. They don't test if their ideas actually work in real company networks or if they break normal business apps. | Hevner et al. (2004); Conti et al. (2018) |
| **Conceptual Gap** | People think of response playbooks as static, step-by-step checklists. They do not see that stopping an attack should be a live problem where you calculate the real business cost of blocking something. | Nepal et al. (2019); MITRE D3FEND™ (2025) |
